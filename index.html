What is CEFR and how can I determine my level? ⇣

The Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) is an international standard for describing language ability on a six-point scale: A1 (beginner), A2 (elementary), B1 (intermediate), B2 (upper intermediate), C1 (advanced), and C2 (proficiency).

How does FlexiRead help improve my reading skills? ⇣

FlexiRead applies Krashen's i+1 principle using advanced AI technology to adapt any text to be precisely one level above your current ability—creating the optimal zone for language acquisition. Our system:

  • Analyzes text using quantifiable linguistic metrics (lexical density, subordination index, T-unit complexity, etc.)
  • Modifies vocabulary and sentence structures while preserving key terminology and original meaning
  • Integrates appropriate scaffolding techniques based on second language acquisition research
  • Evaluates pedagogical value to ensure optimal learning opportunities
  • Verifies the adapted text meets exact CEFR specifications through multi-dimensional analysis

This scientifically calibrated approach ensures you're consistently exposed to language that challenges you appropriately, allowing for natural acquisition of new vocabulary, grammatical structures, and discourse patterns.

How is FlexiRead different from using ChatGPT or other AI tools? ⇣

While general-purpose AI tools can simplify texts, FlexiRead offers several distinct advantages:

  • Precise CEFR calibration: Our system implements detailed linguistic metrics with specific thresholds for each CEFR level (e.g., B1 texts have lexical density of 0.50-0.55, mean sentence length of 10-15 words, etc.)
  • Pedagogical optimization: Beyond linguistic modifications, we evaluate learning objective alignment, noticing facilitation, and scaffolding effectiveness—incorporating principles from second language acquisition research like Schmidt's Noticing Hypothesis and VanPatten's Form-Meaning Connection theory
  • Multi-stage verification: Our specialized pipeline of five AI models ensures both linguistic accuracy and pedagogical value, with the final output reflecting expert consensus rather than a single model's output
  • Research-based approach: Every aspect of FlexiRead is grounded in established linguistic research and language acquisition theory, creating truly optimized learning materials rather than simply "easier" texts
How can I best use FlexiRead to improve my language skills? ⇣

To maximize your learning with FlexiRead:

  • Choose authentic materials that genuinely interest you – motivation is key to sustained language development
  • Compare original and adapted versions to notice vocabulary and structural differences, which helps build awareness of higher-level language patterns
  • Focus on the new vocabulary and grammar structures introduced in the adapted text – these represent your next learning targets
  • Gradually increase your CEFR level as you become comfortable with your current level's adapted texts
  • Read regularly – consistent exposure to comprehensible input at the right level is more effective than occasional intensive study
  • Try reading the original after the adapted version to see how much you can understand with the context and vocabulary you've gained
