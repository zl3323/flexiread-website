FlexiRead Project Reflection

April 28, 2025

Project Overview

Over the past few months, we have been working on building our AI-powered English reading platform, designed to help ESL learners access reading materials tailored to their language proficiency. This project began with a reflection on the limitations of existing leveled reading systems, which often lack diversity and restrict learners from accessing more challenging content. In response, we developed a system that can assess the CEFR levels of articles provided by our users and adapt text difficulty to align with the learner's current stage, following the i+1 principle. We also introduced a CEFR-aligned vocabulary database as a knowledge base to guide text simplification and improve adaptation accuracy.

Throughout the development process, sharing our progress in the Language & Technology Research Group sponsored by Dr. Erik Voss, presenting the platform at Tech Day, and communicating regularly with our project advisor, Dr. Jooeun Shim, provided valuable feedback and insights that helped us continuously refine and strengthen our approach.

Today, our platform has completed its core functionality and is being polished for the final showcase as part of our Integrative Project. Although the project is not commercial at this stage, we are excited about its potential future directions and hope it can continue to support independent language learners beyond the classroom.

Sijia's Reflection

Building this platform has been a journey full of challenges and growth. Text simplification turned out to be more difficult than expected, often requiring multi-stage evaluation to avoid unintentionally increasing complexity. Exploring technical solutions also came with a steep learning curve: it was my first time working with LLMOps platforms like Dify, and learning to design robust AI workflows required continuous experimentation.

To ensure LLMs produced the right type of output at different stages, we spent considerable time refining and modularizing our prompt designs. Balancing linguistic theory (such as i+1 and ZPD) with practical user experience also required constant attention.

In the meantime, I'm especially grateful for the opportunity to work with Zehao—we both held ourselves to high standards, and Zehao's strong project management skills kept the project moving steadily. Working closely together, I not only learned from his organization and planning but also grew my own project management skills through the experience of pushing the project forward collaboratively.

Zehao's Reflection

I was far from being a tech person when I began, and have come a long way to launch FlexiRead. I have some valuable takeaways from this journey that I would like to share with anyone reading this blog.

Important Lessons

DO NOT overlook theories, even if you are designing a product rather than writing a paper. Admittedly, I initially thought this project could be completed through intuition alone—targeting problems I assumed existed and applying strategies I thought might work. In reality, theoretical foundations lay crucial groundwork and provide substantive answers when others inquire about your project.

DO NOT be afraid to learn new things. Both Sijia and I continuously learned throughout this project. From natural language processing and linguistic metrics to large language models, website building, and publishing—every step was significant for us. Once you decide to learn, you will find that making the decision is the hardest part. Resources, tutorials, peers, faculty, and even AI can support you, so do not hesitate to begin.

While I have shared several cautionary notes, I would also like to emphasize:

DO have confidence in yourself and DO trust your partner. I could not have completed this project without Sijia. We both feel grateful for our assiduous, persistent approach and commitment to constant learning. Initially, I was skeptical about the new IP format becoming a group project. Now, I am truly thankful for this opportunity—despite occasional arguments and moments of discouragement, we made it here together!
